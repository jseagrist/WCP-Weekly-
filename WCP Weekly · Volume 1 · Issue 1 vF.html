01The Brief2 min read

A busy week — 7 competitor prints, 5 fund moves.

Healthcare M&A came alive the week of 4/20 — strategics carried it. Sun Pharma/Organon ($11.75B), Lilly/Kelonia ($7B), BioMarin/Amicus ($4.8B), Lilly/Ajax ($2.3B), and Thermo Fisher/Astorg ($1.075B) — five life-sciences deals totaling $26.9B. Across all sectors, $33.9B of capital was announced where deal size was disclosed (16 of 71 deals). The biggest sponsor-side read of the week was the FTC's agreement-in-principle with USAP — a watershed for how PE-backed PPM defenses get framed.

From the competitive funds to WCP: Shore's Dynamic Connections added SAFE Transportation, Oak HC/FT led Courier Health's $50M B at $267M post, WindRose backed Veristat's Certara carve-out, Frazier participated in Egetis's directed share issue, and Council Capital exited Advanced Care Partners to Angels of Care.

Other notable deals / developments: Alcami's acquisition of Tjoapack signals continued pharma services consolidation; Levitate Ridge Capital's CCS/CCOMG oncology partnership is a LMM HC deal worth watching; and Boston-based Haelan Capital Partners, Chicago-based Lincoln Liberty Capital, and Los Angeles-based STA Capital all launched as new HC LMM entrants.

— WCP Research Team
